What Is It

Sepsis is an inflammatory condition characterized by your body’s excessive immune response to a bacterial infection. It often arises from an infected wound or improperly sterilized medical equipment, and is currently the leading cause of death in US hospitals.

What Causes It

Sepsis can emerge from a variety of microogranisms, but the major ones are gram-negative and gram-positive bacteria, and fungi. These microorganisms release toxins that often set off an inflammatory response to try and combat the bacteria. However, unlike a regular infection, this immune response backfires on the body, causing too many cytokines (inflammatory molecules) to be released, leading to severe effects such as fever, organ failure and blood vessel leakage.

How Is It Detected

Diagnosis today often relies upon the expertise of your medical professionals (especially in hospitals located in rural areas)- since there is not a quantitative test or instrument to determine whether a patient has sepsis, and also because the symptoms of sepsis are associated with many other conditions.

How Do We Treat It Today

As soon as a patient enters the intensive care unit (ICU) today, they are given vasopressers, fluids, and broad-range antibiotics in effort to slow down the progression of their inflammation. However, the antibiotics administered at this point are often unable to target the source of the infection. More targeted antibiotic treatment requires a blood test to culture and identify the bacteria which can take several hours- time that most patients do not have when their diagnosis is made too late.

What Do We Propose

SightSepsis is working on a technology to diagnose sepsis by processing insight from a patient’s vital signs in real-time and classifying their condition and its severity. This web application is meant to serve as an intuitive tool for medical professionals and ICU specialists who work with sepsis patients on a daily basis.
